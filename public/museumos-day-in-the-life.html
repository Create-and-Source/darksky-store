International Dark Sky Discovery Center · Fountain Hills, AZ
☼ 7:30 AM
Dr. J Opens the Dashboard
Executive Director

Dr. J logs in from home with his morning coffee. One screen shows him everything — last night's revenue, how many members joined this week, upcoming events, and a message from Maria about a new field trip request from Scottsdale Prep. He doesn't need to check email, open a spreadsheet, or call anyone.

Dr. J checks email, texts Nancy for the revenue number, calls Maria about the field trip, opens a Google Sheet for the events list. 45 minutes gone before he's even dressed.

💬 8:00 AM
Maria Responds to a School
Education Director

Mrs. Rodriguez from Four Peaks Elementary wants to add a rocket-building activity to their April trip. Maria opens Messages and replies in 10 seconds. She then opens Field Trips, clicks the trip, and adds a note. The school can see the update in their School Portal instantly.

Mrs. Rodriguez sent an email three days ago. It's buried under 47 other emails. Maria finds it, replies, then manually updates a Google Doc that Dr. J may or may not check.

🏫 9:00 AM
The School Checks Their Trip Status
School Portal — Mrs. Rodriguez

Mrs. Rodriguez opens the School Portal on her phone. She selects her school from a dropdown — no password needed. She sees her trip is Confirmed, with the date, a preparation checklist (bus parking in Lot B, arrive by 8:45), and Maria's message about the rocket activity. She sends a quick reply: "Perfect, thank you!"

She emails Maria asking for a status update. Maria is in a meeting. She doesn't hear back until tomorrow. She calls the front desk. Sam doesn't know anything about it.

🛍 10:00 AM
Josi Opens the Gift Shop
Gift Shop Manager

Josi signs in and sees 3 low stock alerts on her dashboard. Star map posters are almost out. She opens Purchase Orders, creates a PO to the printer for 50 units, and it's tracked from that moment forward. Then she opens the POS — ready for the first customer.

Josi notices empty shelf space around 2 PM. She texts Nancy "did we reorder the posters?" Nancy says "I thought you did." They call the vendor. It's been out of stock for a week. Lost sales.

⭐ 11:00 AM
A Member Scans Their QR Code
Member Portal + POS

A member walks in and wants to buy a t-shirt. She opens the Member Portal on her phone and shows her QR code. Josi hits Scan on the POS — the camera reads the code and instantly shows: "Member found: Sarah Chen (Explorer — 15% discount)". The discount applies automatically. Josi hits Charge. Done in 30 seconds.

The customer says "I'm a member." Josi asks her name, opens a Google Sheet, scrolls through 200 rows, can't find "Chen" because it was entered as "Sarah C." She gives up and applies the discount manually, hoping it's right. The line grows.

💰 1:00 PM
Nancy Exports to QuickBooks
Treasurer

Nancy signs in and sees her finance dashboard — revenue is up 12% from last month. She opens QuickBooks, clicks "Export Sales (30 Days)" and a perfectly formatted CSV downloads instantly. She drags it into QuickBooks. Done in 2 minutes.

Nancy spends 6 hours at the end of every month manually entering Square receipts into QuickBooks. She types each transaction by hand. She misses three. The books don't balance. She stays late.

🙋 3:00 PM
Carlos Clocks In for His Volunteer Shift
Volunteer Portal

Carlos arrives at the facility and opens the Volunteer Portal on his phone. He taps "Verify My Location" — GPS confirms he's within half a mile of the building. He clocks in. His hours start logging automatically. After his shift, he clocks out and the hours appear in Jordan's Volunteer Management dashboard.

Carlos writes his hours on a paper sign-in sheet. Jordan collects the sheets on Friday, types them into a spreadsheet, and realizes Carlos forgot to sign out twice this week. He texts Carlos. Carlos doesn't remember.

Clear skies tonight. Alex opens Design Studio and types "create a poster for tonight's star party at the Dark Sky Discovery Center." AI generates a graphic in seconds. She exports it, opens Social Media, and schedules posts to Instagram, Facebook, and X. Then she opens Text Blasts, picks the "Clear Sky Alert" template, and sends an SMS to all members: "Clear skies tonight! Observatory open 6-11pm."

Alex asks Maria for the event details. Texts Josi for the Instagram password. Opens Canva, spends 45 minutes on a graphic. Posts to Instagram manually. Forgets Facebook. Nobody texts the members.

The Star Party Fills Up Online
Public Website → Admin Events

Members got the text. Visitors see the event on the website. They click "Reserve Spot," enter their name and email, and they're confirmed. Inside the admin, Sam at the front desk sees the ticket count climbing in real time — 28 of 40 spots filled. He knows exactly how many walk-ins he can still accept.

People call to ask if there's space. Sam checks a paper sign-up sheet. He says "I think so?" Three families drive 45 minutes and get turned away. One of them posts a bad review.

🔭 9:00 PM
A Visitor Becomes a Member
Public Website → CRM

After the star party, a visitor is hooked. She opens the Membership page on her phone, picks the Explorer tier ($49/year), and signs up. Instantly, she appears in the CRM as a new contact with tags: Member, Event Attendee. She gets her Member Portal with a QR code for her next visit. Next time she's in the gift shop, Josi scans her code and she gets 15% off.

The visitor asks about membership. Sam hands her a paper form. She fills it out. Sam puts it in a pile. Nancy enters it into a spreadsheet next week. The visitor comes back before then and doesn't get her discount. She's annoyed.
